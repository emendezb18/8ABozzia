Political Boundaries

During War:

After WWll, the United States and the Soviet Union emerged as the new powers. Due to them, the world was divided in two creating a bipolar system. The war boundaries were known as the Western bloc, and the Eastern bloc. The Western bloc was led by the United States and included democratic states of Western Europe, America, Oceania, and some parts of Asia. The Eastern bloc was led by the Soviet Union and included communist states of Eastern Europe, Asia, and some parts of Africa and Latin America.

After War:

On December 25, 1991, the Soviet Union collapsed. At the end, its collapse resulted in the end of the country, and its federal government causing 15 republics to gain full independence.

While the Soviet Union fell apart, the Western gained more allies. During the war, NATO, which was part of the Western Allies with 16 members, gained a total of 15 new members. After the collapse of the Soviet Union, the Western Union took advantage and started to center on gaining allies from the Eastern bloc.

In addition, the bipolar system ended and a multipolar system started. New powers like China, India and Brazil emerged; and new regional blocs such as the Shanghai Cooperation Organization, and the European Union emerged.

A brief history of the participating countries 15 years after the war

United States:

  • In Sept 11 2001, The World Trade Center Towers fell killing over 70% people in New York City. At the same time causing billions of dollars to be lost, and many markets to close their doors.

  • In March 2003, the United States invaded Iraq with the goal of destroying their weapons of mass destruction and end the rule of Saddam Hussein.

  • In August 2005, a Hurricane known as Katrina caused enormous destruction and loss of life. It caused 1,883 fatalities and 108 billion dollars in damage.

  • Between February and May 2006, around 5 million people took 160 cities across the United States to fight for immigrants rights. The majority of the people fighting were Latinos, European, African, and Asian people.

European Countries:

  • On January 1 2002, the Euro coins were introduced in 12 countries with a total population of around 300 million. Making it the world’s largest monetary changeover.

  • In 2003 heat waves made a record in Europe causing at least 30 thousand deaths. It caused people to be more aware about climate change and global warming.

  • On May 1 2004, the European Union expanded more, gaining 10 countries as new members.

  • Between May 29 - June 1 2005, France and the Netherlands rejected a treaty establishing the constitution for Europe which had already been signed by 25 countries.

  • Between 2006-2007 the EU made an agreement based on the Enlargement Strategy which wanted to stabilize an area of peace and stability sharing common values, prosperity, and competitiveness among all the European countries. It was a priority to maintain security and stability to prevent another future war.

Russia:

  • In the year 1997 Russia signed the NATO-Russia Founding Act, in order to build peace and truce for both Russian and European security.

  • On August 12-13 2000, the Russian nuclear submarine Kursk sank in the Barents Sea. Fortunately, it had not been carrying nuclear charges.

  • May 7,2000 Vladimir Putin became the president of Russia expanding and improving Russia economy and Political power.

  • During May 2002 Putin signed the Treaty of Moscow which was an agreement between the United States and Russia for the reduction of arms including strategic nuclear warheads.

  • In June 2004 Russia made their first Open Skies Treaty observation to the United States military forces and activities.

  • By July 2005 United States and Russia announced an union in order to stop nuclear trafficking, nuclear terrorist incidents, and to improve the security of nuclear weapons.
